What is a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)?

A SIP is a disciplined approach to investing in mutual funds, allowing you to invest a fixed amount regularly (monthly or yearly). It harnesses the power of compounding and rupee cost averaging. Our AI SIP calculator instantly projects your wealth based on expected returns, step-up, and inflation.

Power of Compounding

The earlier you start, the more your money grows. Compounding generates returns on your invested capital and on the accumulated returns. For example, a ₹5,000 monthly SIP at 12% for 20 years can grow to over ₹50 lakhs.

How SIP Returns Are Calculated

We use the standard future value formula for SIP: M = P × [((1+r)^n - 1)/r] × (1+r). Where r = monthly return rate, n = total months. If step-up is added, the investment amount increases annually by the given percentage, reflecting real-world income growth.

What is Step-Up SIP?

Step-up (or top-up) SIP allows you to increase your investment amount every year, usually in line with salary hikes. This tool lets you simulate that and see how it multiplies your final corpus.

Impact of Inflation

Inflation reduces purchasing power. By entering an inflation rate, we show the inflation-adjusted maturity value, so you know the real worth of your future corpus in today's rupees.
